# 📘 Lesson Notes – Present Perfect Continuous Tense (Grade 7–9)

---

# 🌟 What is the Present Perfect Continuous Tense?

The **Present Perfect Continuous Tense** is used to talk about:

* An action that **started in the past and is still continuing**.
* An action that **started in the past and has recently stopped**, but its effect can still be seen.
* The **duration** of an activity.

---

## ✨ Examples

* She has been studying for two hours.
* They have been playing football since 4 p.m.
* I have been waiting for you.
* We have been learning English for six months.

---

# 📚 Structure of Present Perfect Continuous Tense

## ✅ Positive Sentences

### Structure

> **Subject + has/have + been + Verb-ing**

### Examples

| Subject | Sentence                             |
| ------- | ------------------------------------ |
| I       | I have been reading a book.          |
| We      | We have been learning English.       |
| You     | You have been working hard.          |
| They    | They have been playing football.     |
| He      | He has been studying since morning.  |
| She     | She has been cooking for an hour.    |
| It      | It has been raining since yesterday. |

---

# 📝 Since vs For

## Use **Since** for the **starting point** of an action.

### Examples

* since 8 a.m.
* since Monday
* since January
* since 2022
* since morning

### Sentences

* She has been studying **since 8 a.m.**
* They have been living here **since 2020.**

---

## Use **For** for the **duration** of an action.

### Examples

* for 10 minutes
* for two hours
* for three days
* for six months
* for many years

### Sentences

* He has been working **for five hours.**
* We have been learning English **for six months.**

---

# ❌ Negative Sentences

### Structure

> **Subject + has/have + not + been + Verb-ing**

### Examples

* She has not been studying.
* They have not been playing football.
* I have not been feeling well.
* We have not been waiting long.

---

# ❓ Yes/No Questions

### Structure

> **Has/Have + Subject + been + Verb-ing?**

### Examples

* Have you been studying?
* Have they been working?
* Has she been reading?
* Has he been sleeping?

---

# ❓ Wh Questions

Wh Questions begin with:

* What
* Where
* When
* Why
* Who
* How

### Structure

> **Wh Word + has/have + Subject + been + Verb-ing?**

---

## Examples

### What

* What have you been doing?
* What has she been reading?

### Where

* Where have they been working?
* Where has he been staying?

### When

* When have you been waiting?

### Why

* Why have they been laughing?
* Why has she been crying?

### How

* How have you been feeling?

### Who

* Who has been teaching this class?

---

# ❓ Negative Yes/No Questions

### Structure

> **Hasn't/Haven't + Subject + been + Verb-ing?**

### Examples

* Hasn't he been studying?
* Haven't they been waiting?
* Hasn't she been working?

---

# ❓ Negative Wh Questions

### Structure

> **Wh Word + hasn't/haven't + Subject + been + Verb-ing?**

### Examples

* Why hasn't he been studying?
* Why haven't they been coming to school?
* What hasn't she been doing?

---

# 📚 Uses of Present Perfect Continuous Tense

---

## 1️⃣ Actions Started in the Past and Still Continuing

* She has been studying for three hours.
* They have been living in Bengaluru since 2020.

---

## 2️⃣ Recently Stopped Actions with Present Results

* I am tired because I have been running.
* Her hands are dirty because she has been gardening.

---

## 3️⃣ To Show Duration

* We have been waiting for an hour.
* He has been working since morning.

---

# 📝 Common Time Expressions

Present Perfect Continuous is commonly used with:

* since
* for
* all day
* all morning
* lately
* recently
* for a long time

### Examples

* She has been reading **for two hours**.
* We have been waiting **since 9 a.m.**
* It has been raining **all day**.

---

# ⚠️ Common Mistakes

### Incorrect:

* She has been study for two hours.

### Correct:

* She has been studying for two hours.

---

### Incorrect:

* They have been played football.

### Correct:

* They have been playing football.

---

### Incorrect:

* Has she been reading a book.

### Correct:

* Has she been reading a book?

---

### Incorrect:

* He has been working since two hours.

### Correct:

* He has been working for two hours.

---

### Incorrect:

* She has been studying for Monday.

### Correct:

* She has been studying since Monday.

---

# 📝 Present Perfect vs Present Perfect Continuous

| Present Perfect                     | Present Perfect Continuous                          |
| ----------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| Focuses on the **completed action** | Focuses on the **duration of the action**           |
| She has written three letters.      | She has been writing letters for two hours.         |
| They have finished the project.     | They have been working on the project since Monday. |

# ✏️ Practice Exercise 1

2. They ______ ______ ______ (play) football since 4 p.m.

3. I ______ ______ ______ (wait) for the bus.

4. We ______ ______ ______ (learn) English for six months.

1. has been studying

2. have been playing
