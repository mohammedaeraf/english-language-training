# 📘 Lesson Notes – Punctuation Rules (Grade 7–9)

---

# 🌟 What is Punctuation?

**Punctuation** refers to the symbols used in writing to make sentences clear, meaningful, and easy to read.

Just as traffic signals guide vehicles on roads, punctuation marks guide readers through a sentence.

### Example:

❌ lets eat grandma

✅ Let's eat, Grandma!

Notice how punctuation completely changes the meaning.

---

# 📚 Common Punctuation Marks

| Punctuation Mark | Symbol | Purpose                          |
| ---------------- | ------ | -------------------------------- |
| Full Stop        | .      | Ends a statement                 |
| Question Mark    | ?      | Ends a question                  |
| Exclamation Mark | !      | Shows strong feeling             |
| Comma            | ,      | Separates words or ideas         |
| Apostrophe       | '      | Shows possession or contraction  |
| Quotation Marks  | " "    | Show direct speech               |
| Colon            | :      | Introduces a list or explanation |
| Semicolon        | ;      | Joins related sentences          |

---

# 1️⃣ Full Stop (.)

Use a full stop at the end of a statement.

### Examples:

- She is studying English.
- We visited Bengaluru last week.
- The sun rises in the east.

---

# 2️⃣ Question Mark (?)

Use a question mark after a question.

### Examples:

- What is your name?
- Are you ready?
- Where do you live?

---

# 3️⃣ Exclamation Mark (!)

Use an exclamation mark to show:

- excitement
- surprise
- joy
- anger

### Examples:

- Wow! That is amazing!
- Hurray! We won the match!
- Ouch! That hurt!

---

# 4️⃣ Comma (,)

Use commas to separate:

- items in a list
- introductory words
- extra information

### Examples:

### In a list:

- I bought apples, bananas, oranges, and grapes.

### After introductory words:

- Yes, I agree with you.
- However, the plan failed.

### Extra information:

- Aarif, our class monitor, helped the teacher.

---

# 5️⃣ Apostrophe (')

## A. To Show Possession

### Examples:

- Rahul's bag
- The dog's tail
- The teacher's desk

---

## B. In Contractions

### Examples:

- do not → don't
- is not → isn't
- cannot → can't
- I am → I'm

---

# 6️⃣ Quotation Marks (" ")

Use quotation marks for direct speech.

### Examples:

- The teacher said, "Open your notebooks."
- Ali asked, "Can I leave early?"

---

# 7️⃣ Colon (:)

Use a colon before:

- a list
- an explanation

### Examples:

- Bring the following items: pen, pencil, ruler, and notebook.
- She had one goal: to win the competition.

---

# 8️⃣ Semicolon (;)

Use a semicolon to join closely related sentences.

### Examples:

- It was raining; we stayed indoors.
- She studied hard; therefore, she passed the exam.

---

# 📝 Capitalization Rules

Always begin a sentence with a capital letter.

### Examples:

❌ my name is Rahul.

✅ My name is Rahul.

---

Use capital letters for:

- Names of people
- Cities
- Countries
- Days
- Months

### Examples:

- Aarif
- Mangalore
- India
- Monday
- January

---

# ⚠️ Common Mistakes

### Incorrect:

- where are you going

### Correct:

- Where are you going?

---

### Incorrect:

- i live in india.

### Correct:

- I live in India.

---

### Incorrect:

- wow that is beautiful

### Correct:

- Wow! That is beautiful.

# ✏️ Practice Exercise 1 – Add the Correct Punctuation

Rewrite the following sentences with proper punctuation.

1. where do you live

2. wow that is a beautiful painting

3. my favourite fruits are apples bananas oranges and grapes

4. the teacher said open your books

5. i visited mysuru last sunday

---

# ✅ Answers

1. Where do you live?

2. Wow! That is a beautiful painting!

3. My favourite fruits are apples, bananas, oranges, and grapes.

4. The teacher said, "Open your books."

5. I visited Mysuru last Sunday.
