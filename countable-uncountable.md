# 📘 Countable vs Uncountable Nouns

## 1️⃣ Countable Nouns

These are nouns that can be **counted**.

They have:

- Singular and plural forms
- Numbers before them

### Examples:

- one book → two books
- one apple → five apples
- one student → many students

### Words used with Countable Nouns:

- many
- few
- a few
- several

### Sentences:

- I have **three pens**.
- There are **many students** in the class.

---

## 2️⃣ Uncountable Nouns

These are nouns that **cannot be counted individually**.

They usually:

- Do not have plural forms
- Are treated as singular

### Examples:

- water
- rice
- sugar
- milk
- information
- furniture

### Words used with Uncountable Nouns:

- much
- little
- a little
- some

### Sentences:

- We need **some water**.
- There is **little sugar** left.

---

# ⚠️ Important Note

We do not say:
❌ two waters
❌ many furniture

We say:
✅ two bottles of water
✅ much furniture

---

---

# 🎯 Easy Trick

Ask:

> “Can I count it one by one?”

- Yes → Countable
- No → Uncountable

Examples:

- 1 apple, 2 apples ✅
- 1 water, 2 waters ❌
