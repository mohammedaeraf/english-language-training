# 📘 Verbs

## 🌟 What is a Verb?

A **verb** is a word that shows:

- an **action**
- a **state**
- or an **occurrence**

A verb tells us what someone or something **does**.

---

## ✨ Examples

### Action Verbs:

- run
- eat
- write
- play

### State Verbs:

- is
- am
- are
- seem

### Sentences:

- The children **played** football.
- She **is** happy.
- Birds **fly** in the sky.

---

# 📚 Types of Verbs

---

## 1️⃣ Action Verbs

These show physical or mental action.

### Examples:

- jump
- read
- think
- laugh

### Sentences:

- He **runs** every morning.
- I **thought** about the answer.

---

## 2️⃣ Linking Verbs

These connect the subject to more information.

### Common Linking Verbs:

- is
- am
- are
- was
- were
- seem
- become

### Sentences:

- She **is** a doctor.
- The soup **smells** delicious.

---

## 3️⃣ Helping Verbs (Auxiliary Verbs)

These help the main verb.

### Examples:

- is
- have
- do
- can
- will
- should

### Sentences:

- She **is reading** a book.
- They **have completed** the work.
- I **can swim**.

---

## 4️⃣ Transitive Verbs

These verbs need an object to complete the meaning.

### Examples:

- eat
- kick
- write

### Sentences:

- He kicked the **ball**.
- She wrote a **letter**.

(“ball” and “letter” are objects.)

---

## 5️⃣ Intransitive Verbs

These verbs do not need an object.

### Examples:

- sleep
- laugh
- arrive

### Sentences:

- The baby slept.
- Everyone laughed.

---

# 📝 Verb Forms

| Base Form | Past Form | Past Participle |
| --------- | --------- | --------------- |
| go        | went      | gone            |
| eat       | ate       | eaten           |
| write     | wrote     | written         |
| play      | played    | played          |

---

# ⏰ Verbs and Tenses

Verbs change form according to time.

| Tense   | Example         |
| ------- | --------------- |
| Present | She writes.     |
| Past    | She wrote.      |
| Future  | She will write. |

---

# 🎯 Remember

Every complete sentence usually needs a verb.

### Example:

❌ The boy in the park.
✅ The boy **is playing** in the park.
