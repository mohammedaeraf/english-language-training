# 📘 Lesson Notes – Sentence and Its Types (Grade 7–9)

---

# 🌟 What is a Sentence?

A **sentence** is a group of words that makes complete sense.

A sentence usually contains:

* a **subject**
* and a **verb**

---

## ✨ Examples

* He is in the bedroom.
* She has gone to the market.
* They are playing football.

---

# 📚 Types of Sentences

---

# 1️⃣ Positive / Affirmative Sentences

These sentences state something positively.

## Structure:

> Subject + Verb + Object/Complement

## Examples:

* He is studying.
* They are studying.
* I am studying.
* She will come tomorrow.

---

# 2️⃣ Negative Sentences

These sentences express denial or negation.

We usually add:

* not
* do not / does not
* will not
* cannot

## Examples:

* He is not studying.
* They are not studying.
* She will not come tomorrow.

---

# 3️⃣ Yes/No Questions

These questions can be answered with:

* Yes
  or
* No

## Structure:

> Helping Verb + Subject + Main Verb?

## Examples:

* Is he studying?
* Are they studying?
* Will she come tomorrow?

---

# 4️⃣ Wh Questions

These questions begin with:

* What
* Where
* When
* Why
* How
* How many
* How much

They ask for specific information.

## Examples:

* What is he studying?
* Where is he studying?
* How is he studying?
* When will she come?

---

# 5️⃣ Negative Yes/No Questions

These are Yes/No questions in negative form.

## Examples:

* Isn't he studying?
* Aren't they going?
* Won't she come tomorrow?

---

# 6️⃣ Negative Wh Questions

These are Wh Questions in negative form.

## Examples:

* Why isn't he studying?
* Why aren't they going?
* Why won't she come tomorrow?

---

# 📝 Sentence Transformation Examples

| Positive Sentence       | Negative Sentence           | Yes/No Question         | Wh Question                  |
| ----------------------- | --------------------------- | ----------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| He is studying.         | He is not studying.         | Is he studying?         | What is he studying?         |
| They are playing.       | They are not playing.       | Are they playing?       | Where are they playing?      |
| She will come tomorrow. | She will not come tomorrow. | Will she come tomorrow? | When will she come tomorrow? |

---

# ⚠️ Important Rules

## ✅ Rule 1

In questions, the helping verb comes before the subject.

### Example:

* She is studying.
* Is she studying?

---

## ✅ Rule 2

Use “not” to make negative sentences.

### Example:

* They are playing.
* They are not playing.

---

## ✅ Rule 3

Wh Questions begin with question words.

### Example:

* Where are they going?
* Why is he crying?

---

# ✏️ Practice Exercise

Write:

1. Negative Sentence
2. Yes/No Question
3. Wh Question

for each sentence below.

---

## Questions

1. They are playing in the garden.

2. We are going to Mangalore.

3. She is learning AI.

4. Aarif has completed the homework.

5. They have won the match.

6. She will come tomorrow.

---

# ✅ Solutions

---

## 1. They are playing in the garden.

* Negative: They are not playing in the garden.
* Yes/No Question: Are they playing in the garden?
* Wh Question: Why are they playing in the garden?

---

## 2. We are going to Mangalore.

* Negative: We are not going to Mangalore.
* Yes/No Question: Are we going to Mangalore?
* Wh Question: Where are we going?

---

## 3. She is learning AI.

* Negative: She is not learning AI.
* Yes/No Question: Is she learning AI?
* Wh Question: What is she learning?

---

## 4. Aarif has completed the homework.

* Negative: Aarif has not completed the homework.
* Yes/No Question: Has Aarif completed the homework?
* Wh Question: What has Aarif completed?

---

## 5. They have won the match.

* Negative: They have not won the match.
* Yes/No Question: Have they won the match?
* Wh Question: What have they won?

---

## 6. She will come tomorrow.

* Negative: She will not come tomorrow.
* Yes/No Question: Will she come tomorrow?
* Wh Question: When will she come tomorrow?
