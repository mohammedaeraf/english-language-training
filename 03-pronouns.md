# 📘 Pronouns

## 🌟 What is a Pronoun?

A **pronoun** is a word used **instead of a noun** to avoid repetition.

### Example:

❌ Riya is a good student. Riya studies well.
✅ Riya is a good student. **She** studies well.

Here, **She** is a pronoun.

---

# 📚 Types of Pronouns

---

## 1️⃣ Personal Pronouns

These refer to specific persons or things.

| Subject Pronouns | Object Pronouns |
| ---------------- | --------------- |
| I                | me              |
| we               | us              |
| you              | you             |
| he               | him             |
| she              | her             |
| it               | it              |
| they             | them            |

### Examples:

* **She** is singing.
* The teacher called **him**.

---

## 2️⃣ Possessive Pronouns

These show ownership or possession.

### Examples:

* mine
* yours
* his
* hers
* ours
* theirs

### Sentences:

* This bag is **mine**.
* That house is **theirs**.

---

## 3️⃣ Demonstrative Pronouns

These point to specific people or things.

### Examples:

* this
* that
* these
* those

### Sentences:

* **This** is my notebook.
* **Those** are beautiful flowers.

---

## 4️⃣ Reflexive Pronouns

These refer back to the subject.

### Examples:

* myself
* yourself
* himself
* herself
* themselves

### Sentences:

* She completed the work **herself**.
* I hurt **myself** while playing.

---

## 5️⃣ Interrogative Pronouns

These are used to ask questions.

### Examples:

* who
* whom
* whose
* which
* what

### Sentences:

* **Who** is your class teacher?
* **Which** is your bag?

---

## 6️⃣ Indefinite Pronouns

These refer to people or things in a general way.

### Examples:

* someone
* everyone
* nobody
* many
* few

### Sentences:

* **Everyone** enjoyed the trip.
* **Someone** knocked at the door.

---

# 📝 Quick Comparison Table

| Type          | Purpose                  | Examples        |
| ------------- | ------------------------ | --------------- |
| Personal      | Refers to persons/things | he, she, they   |
| Possessive    | Shows ownership          | mine, yours     |
| Demonstrative | Points out               | this, those     |
| Reflexive     | Refers back to subject   | myself, herself |
| Interrogative | Asks questions           | who, what       |
| Indefinite    | General reference        | someone, many   |

---

# 🎯 Why Pronouns Are Important

Pronouns:

* Make sentences shorter
* Avoid repetition
* Improve speaking and writing
